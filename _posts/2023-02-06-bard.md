Google has just <a href="https://blog.google/technology/ai/bard-google-ai-search-updates/" target="_blank">announced</a> its new AI language model for dialogue applications called Bard, which is based on its highly-acclaimed Lambda model. For those who are not familiar with Lambda, it's a model that has sparked controversy after a Google engineer claimed that Lambda had become sentient.

However, Google has since given a demo of Lambda and is now opening up access to trusted testers before making it widely available to the public in the coming weeks. With Bard, Google positions itself as a charge GPT competitor, showcasing the latest information available from 2023.

One of the interesting aspects of Bard is that Google is emphasizing the importance of being bold and responsible in AI development, making it a safe and trustworthy tool for users. This focus on ethical AI is becoming more common among tech giants, but it is great to see Google leading the charge.

The demo of Bard shows that it can help simplify complex topics by answering questions, just like charge GPT. This competition is extremely healthy for the AI ecosystem and it's exciting to see Google getting serious about conversational AI.
